Josh Decker is a trumpet performer and educator currently residing in central Ohio. His playing career has been both versatile and rewarding, performing in prestigious groups in central Ohio and throughout the United States.

Josh serves as 3rd trumpet with the Newark-Granville Symphony Orchestra and performs as a section player with the Westerville Symphony Orchestra. Other orchestral highlights have included performances with Opera Project Columbus and Carnegie Mellon Philharmonic, where he had the pleasure of playing repertoire under the batons of conductors David Effron, Carl Topilow and Pittsburgh Symphony's Manfred Honeck.

Other career highlights include extensive work with Columbus' Short North Stage Musical Company, Bach Choir of Pittsburgh, and a three week residency at the children's theme park, Dutch Wonderland in Lancaster, PA.

Josh's musical interests and expertise lead him to co-founding Pittsburgh's brass-funk band, the Employees of Funk, a group in which he composed original material, arrangements, and played trumpet in from 2015-2017.

As a soloist, Josh performed the National Anthem at the NCAA track and field national championships in 2013.

As an educator, Josh has had a massive reach in central Ohio, currently teaching talented musicians in the Gahanna, Dublin, and Olentangy school districts ranging from 8-18 years of age.

Numerous students of Josh's have had success at OMEA Solo and Ensemble competitions, as well as hold spots in the city's best youth ensembles, including Columbus Youth Symphony Orchestra, Columbus Youth Jazz Orchestra and the Columbus Youth Symphonic Band.

In the Summer of 2019, Josh directed the Music Royale Brass Camp, an immersive week-long camp that exposed young brass musicians to a multi-genre ensemble experience.

Josh holds a BM in Music Education from Ohio Wesleyan University and a Masters of Music in trumpet performance from Carnegie Mellon University. His primary teachers include Larry Griffin and George Vosburgh, former Principal Trumpet of the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra.
